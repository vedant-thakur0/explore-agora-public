# AGORA Tags vs. Party Alignment Analysis

## Executive Summary

**Key Finding**: Strong partisan splits on AI policy tags, with Democrats dominating **21/30** surveyed tags (70%), while Republicans concentrate on a single tag: **"Strategies: Input controls"** (79.8% GOP).

**Implication**: Democratic AI policy focus emphasizes **disclosure, evaluation, harm mitigation** (transparency-driven). Republican focus centers on **data/compute controls** (regulation-skeptical).

## Policy Implications

### 1. **AI Safety Bills Are Democratic Territory**
- Tags like "bias," "discrimination," "safety" are 80-93% Democratic
- Republicans can't ignore (they do cosponsor at 7-20%), but Democrats set agenda
- **Strategy for Republicans**: Frame as cost/competitiveness risk, not safety

### 2. **Input Controls Are Republican Differentiator**
- Only tag with GOP majority (80%)
- Concentrated in international affairs / defense committees
- Driven by China competition concerns, not domestic AI regulation
- **Strategy for Democrats**: Frame as economic isolation, not security

### 3. **R&D/Investment is Common Ground**
- "Gov support" (57% Dem) and "Gov support: For R&D" (60% Dem) are closest to balanced
- Both parties want funding; disagree on conditions/accountability
- **Strategy for coalition builders**: Lead with investment, follow with guardrails

### 4. **Bipartisan Bills Exist But Are Narrow**
- Armed Forces (defense), some tech R&D, some liability frameworks
- Typically avoid evaluation/transparency clauses (Democratic)
- Typically avoid input controls (Republican)
- **Implication**: Bipartisan bills are process-neutral or infrastructure-focused

## Limitations

- **Cosponsor selection bias**: Early cosponsors may be more ideological; later ones bandwagoning
- **Multi-tag bills**: Tags often co-occur; can't isolate individual tag effect
- **Temporal: 118th Congress only**: Pattern may shift with Democratic/Republican majorities
- **Missing context**: No floor votes, amendments, committee votes; just sponsorship

---

## Key Takeaway

**AI policy in Congress shows a 70-93% Democratic dominance on transparency/rights/evaluation, opposed by a 80% Republican position on input controls (international competition). Bipartisan ground is narrow: R&D funding and process-neutral infrastructure.**

This partisan structure explains why AI bills either pass with Democratic-led coalitions (with Republican fringe support) or are narrowly defined to avoid evaluation/transparency (defense, investment-focused).
